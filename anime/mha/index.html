World Overview

The world of My Hero Academia was once similar to ours until a baby was born with the ability to glow. Soon after, humans began to manifest special abilities known as "Quirks," plunging the world into chaos. Now, stability and peace have been restored, with 80% of the population possessing Quirks.

The use of Quirks in public is generally prohibited to ensure civilian safety. Those who use their gifts for selfish gain are branded as "Villains." In contrast, "Heroes" are formalized civil servants who use their Quirks to protect the populace, assist in disaster relief, and collaborate with police to solve crimes.

All Might, the greatest hero, is the "Pillar of Peace" responsible for the current golden age. However, his critical injuries mean he will soon retire, and a rising darkness, led by charismatic villains like All For One, threatens society's fragile peace.

The story begins ten months before U.A. High's next Entrance Exam, coinciding with All Might's first meeting with Izuku Midoriya, a Quirkless boy aspiring to be a hero.

Characters

Heroes

Professional heroes, licensed to use their Quirks for good. They are public figures, highly scrutinized, and undergo rigorous training at schools like U.A. High.

Villains

Individuals who use their Quirks for illegal acts. They range from hardened criminals to those seeking liberation from societal norms.

Students

Young individuals aspiring to greatness, often aiming for hero schools like U.A. High, Ketsubutsu Academy, or Shiketsu High.

Support

Individuals involved in the support industry, designing costumes, gadgets, and managing PR for heroes and villains.

Vigilantes

Those who take a stand against crime outside the established law enforcement and hero system.

Revolutionaries

Individuals with strong opinions about the world's direction, willing to change it themselves, often defying the status quo.

Quirks

Quirks are innate powers manifesting between birth and age four, behaving like a natural bodily function. They can be trained or atrophy if neglected, and often have natural limitations.

Quirks are categorized by type and price tier. There are four tiers, from weakest/cheapest (Tier 0) to strongest/most expensive (Tier 3). Nomu can purchase multiple Quirks, while others are limited to one.

Quirk Types:

Manipulation Quirks

Control external substances, elements, or objects (e.g., fire, earth, bullets). Can control more, better, and longer with training. Limited by quantity, control precision, stamina, and availability of the substance.

Generation Quirks

Produce something from the user's body (e.g., acid, black holes). Often esoteric with visible signs of generation. Training strengthens production, expands "fuel reservoir," and improves control. Limited by control after generation, drawbacks from overuse, and sustainability.

Augmentation Quirks

Manipulate, enhance, and control one's own body. Vary in complexity and scope. Training pushes them farther or reveals new tricks. Limitations often center on the health of affected body parts.

Alteration Quirks

Strange close-range Quirks affecting how things work (e.g., disintegrating objects, shutting off gravity). Can be physically demanding or hard to control. Training makes them more powerful but rarely more flexible. Limitations include being "always on" or causing side effects like headaches.

Affliction Quirks

Focused on hindering, affecting, or controlling others, with few personal advantages. Often require specific conditions (e.g., consuming blood, verbal response). Training is difficult due to reliance on targets.

Mutation Quirks

Permanent bodily features (e.g., muscular tail, horns). Behave like natural body parts. Training improves traditional aspects like strength, dexterity, or endurance. Limitations include muscle fatigue and natural limitations of the body part.

Sensory Quirks

Expand or improve perception and communication (e.g., enhanced sight, new senses). Training makes them more acute or expands range. Limitations are typically related to range of perception or precision of the sense.

Perks & Items

Gain new abilities, tools, and advantages. Perks provide innate skills and enhancements, while items offer equipment and resources. Origins offer discounts on matching perks and items.

Nomu have unique perk-lines and item selections, allowing for more specialized builds.

Key Perks:

  • Power Lottery (Free!): Create a custom Quirk by fusing up to four existing Quirks (parents/grandparents), or modify an existing Quirk's tier and properties.
  • Quirk Proficiency (200CP): Gain extensive training and experience with your Quirk, understanding its nuances, strengths, and weaknesses.
  • Superficial Mutant (Free): Customize your appearance to be highly mutated, or very close to a traditional human, with minor limitations.
  • Real Life Ninja (300CP): Gain inhuman agility, acrobatic acumen, hand-to-hand combat skills, proficiency with conventional ninja weaponry, and one ninja technique.

Key Items:

  • All-Might Jacket (50CP): A limited edition, mint condition jacket resembling All Might's costume, unable to be dirtied or damaged, making you look cool to nerds.
  • Guns (50CP/200CP): Purchase a gun of your choice with a large amount of ammunition, or an entire arsenal with consistent restock.
  • Industrial Support Workshop (400CP): A fully equipped workshop for designing, making, and testing support items for heroes and villains.
  • Villain Factory (600CP): A small but well-trained operation with multiple safehouses for abduction, human experimentation with Quirk Enhancement Drugs (Trigger), and securing contraband.

Scenarios & Drawbacks

Scenarios offer unique challenges and rewards, allowing you to shape your story within the My Hero Academia world. Drawbacks add difficulty for extra CP.

DIY Scenario Creation:

You can create your own scenario with a setup (background alteration), a narrative (ordeals to overcome), and a goal (reward). The scale of changes and difficulty match the reward.

  • Example: All For One (Quirk) Reward: Setup as an orphan manipulated by All For One, narrative mirrors Shigaraki's path of cultivating hatred and fighting heroes/villains, goal of overcoming AFO's attempted possession and embodying your villainous ideal.
  • Example: One For All (Quirk) Reward: Setup and narrative mirror Midoriya's, aspiring hero receiving One For All early. Goal is to become the number one hero and change society for the better to keep the Quirk.
  • Casual Scenarios: Smaller rewards like romance with a character or a steady income for your family are also possible, leading to a more comfortable narrative.

Drawbacks:

  • Origin Story (+0): Roll back your start up to twenty years prior to current events.
  • Vigilantes! (+0): Start a year or two earlier, at the beginning of the 'Vigilante: Boku no Hero Academia Illegals' spinoff series.
  • Fanfiction (+0): Select any existing fanfic as the baseline for your experiences.
  • Wild Nomu (-): (Specific to Nomu Origin) Tiered drawback based on number and tier of Quirks, ranging from no complications to actively being hunted by All For One and the Doctor.
  • Bitter Rivalry (+100CP): Gain a rival you can't permanently get rid of; careers always draw you together, attempts to kill them fail, persistent annoyance.
  • Quirkless (+200CP): No Quirk for the duration of your stay, treated as a second-class citizen. (Mutually incompatible with Quirk-driven drawbacks).

Heroes

Professional heroes are licensed individuals who use their Quirks to protect the populace, assist in disaster relief, and collaborate with police to solve crimes. They are public figures, constantly in the spotlight, and undergo rigorous training at specialized schools like U.A. High, known as the #1 Hero Academy in the world.

All Might, the "Pillar of Peace," is the most iconic hero, inspiring both civilians and fellow heroes while striking fear into villains. Becoming a professional hero is extremely difficult due to the intense scrutiny and responsibility involved.

Villains

Villains are individuals who use their Quirks to perform illegal acts. Some are hardened criminals, while others act out of a desire for public liberation and defiance of the law. Their treatment by society varies based on their actions and location.

Despite the established hero system, crime persists, and the era of peace is fragile with All Might's impending retirement. Villains like All For One are plotting to assert dominance, and the Villain Alliance is growing stronger.

Villain Perks:

  • Slasher's Smile (100CP): Project malicious intent, psychotic glee, or unsettling feelings with your smile/teeth.
  • Not A 'Bad' Guy (100CP): Social skills to get on the good side of bad people, navigating conversations and negotiations.
  • Runaway Getaway (200CP): Impeccable at navigating the underbelly of society and covering tracks, with contacts for illegal 'work' and 'markets'.
  • Dead in the Water (200CP): Improvise and set up traps on short notice, skilled at baiting and luring targets.
  • Self-Mutilation (400CP): Keen awareness of body limitations, ability to self-harm without shock, and use painful Quirks while staying aware of limits.

  • Modern Moriarty (600CP): Masterful schemer, layering plans, thriving through 'failure,' and enduring blows to your enterprise.
  • Demon Lord (600CP): Lucid 'Quirk Ghost' attached to your biology, fighting foreign abilities and exerting influence, fueled by intense emotion (especially hatred).

Students

Young individuals, often in middle school, with aspirations of becoming heroes. They typically have their sights set on entrance exams for prestigious hero schools like U.A. High, Ketsubutsu Academy, or Shiketsu High. Their families usually provide unwavering emotional and financial support for their heroic endeavors.

For those who choose a different path, or a normal life, their families remain supportive. Hero work is dangerous, so safety is a concern for loved ones.

Support

The Support Industry is a broad term for all trades and services catering to the needs of a Hero (or Villain). This includes managers for PR and merchandise, tailors for protective and stylish costumes, and engineers who design gadgets. They are the brains behind the operation, providing crucial assistance to those on the front lines.

Support Perks:

  • Quick Study (100CP): Skilled at scanning, absorbing, and applying information quickly, essential for catering to individual hero needs.
  • Passion For Fashion (100CP): Talented artisan in costume design, creating functional and fashionable looks that bring out an individual's personality, working with various materials including bodily ones.
  • Public Relations (200CP): Remarkable skill in improving public perception, with a silver tongue for managing media and guiding conversations.
  • Obsession (200CP): Irrational investment in goals, drawing on sheer focus, determination, and stamina for tireless work.
  • Fixation (400CP): General intelligence surpassing peers, with a particular fixation of choice leading to unrealistic expertise (e.g., atomic structure, support tool ideas, hacking).
  • Industry Support (400CP): Competence in diverse fields (material sciences, mechanical engineering), talented at designing tools that efficiently elevate client powers/skills and expand options.
  • Tribute (600CP): Pinnacle of support, able to emulate a wide variety of Quirks through technological means (robotic limbs, lasers, modular armor).
  • The Doctor Is In (600CP): Foremost expert on Quirk biological research, with knowledge to replicate Quirks through cloning or surgically augment bodies to withstand Quirk use.

Manipulation Quirks Examples

Manipulation Quirks are all about controlling some external substance, element or object. As they are trained, users can usually control more, better and for longer.

Tier 0:

  • Shadow Puppets: Manipulate your own shadow's form.
  • Season City: Manipulate common flavor enhancers like salt, sugar, pepper, ketchup, or mustard.
  • Headlights: Collect and manipulate photons into linear beams or conical rays.
  • Airburst: Compress small quantities of air into marble-shaped pockets and project them.
  • Leaf Manipulation: Control leaves from surroundings and project them as sharpened blades.
  • Weak Attraction: Pull small, lightweight objects closer.
  • Confetti: Manipulate pieces of paper after contact, shaping and splitting them.
  • Curveball: Apply a delayed rotation to small objects, designating direction.
  • Snow Shoes: Disperse exerted forces of body over a larger area for easier movement on soft surfaces or lifting awkward objects.
  • Placebo: Create small, strangely patterned pills that make others more suggestible.

Tier 1:

  • Anivoice: Communicate with and command animals.
  • Babel: Auto-telekinesis, commanding inanimate objects to stack into perfectly balanced towers.
  • Homing: Lock onto an opponent, causing projectiles to home in on them.
  • Echo: Control and collect nearby sounds into music note constructs that bounce around.
  • Signal Pirate: Hijack electronic signals to manipulate audio and video on nearby electronics.
  • Larceny: Steal any objects seen on a target's person, including non-permanent Quirk manifestations.
  • Size: Increase the size of objects you've interacted with up to several dozen times over.
  • Storage: Take in and store objects inside a separate space within your body.
  • Boomerang: Channel energy into objects, accelerating and altering their trajectory after throwing.
  • Anthropomorph: Transform roughly human-sized objects into controllable puppets.

Tier 2:

  • Fiber Master: Freely control and manipulate the fibers making up textiles.
  • Water Accumulation: Exert extremely fine control over water, even pulling moisture from the air.
  • Repulsion: Manipulate a repulsive, invisible force that bounces anything away.
  • Cement: Power over cement and cement-like materials, able to expand, 'grow,' or repair them.
  • Handyman: Mentally grab and manipulate pieces of a broken object, regenerating damage.
  • Extend-o-Hair: Incredible control over your own body hair, growing and manipulating it for offense, defense, and utility.
  • Dust Bunny: Powerful, highly refined control over dust and similar particulates over a wide area.
  • Flight: Manipulate a special aerodynamic barrier for high-speed flight.
  • Mummification: Produce red bandages to control and transform inanimate objects into mummy-like golems.
  • Magic: Create dazzling rings of light that expand into platform-like constructs to move people or objects.

Tier 3:

  • Earthflow: Exert great control over large amounts of earthen substances, creating Earth Devils.
  • Meatball: Manipulate and change the shape of raw human flesh, compressing people into ball-shaped lumps.
  • Soundgarden: Manipulate large sums of sand and glass through song, transforming sand into glass and vice versa.
  • Bottled Lightning: Manipulate electricity with fine control, pulling it from surroundings.
  • Fungal Growth: Detect, manipulate, condense, and rapidly grow fungus or fungal growth, producing spores.
  • Maximum Security: Great control over iron and iron alloys, commanding it over a wide area, forming bars and chains.
  • Canopic Jar: Separate organs from body, body becomes redundant and can regenerate around bone jar.
  • Weather Manipulation: Freely manipulate weather over a region, creating storms, lightning, or aiding flight.
  • New Order: Set a "rule" upon something by touching it and calling its name, manipulating targets or changing properties.
  • Ruin: Loosen chemical bonds of objects seen, radically lowering durability and allowing breaking through tough materials.

Generation Quirks Examples

Generation Quirks are all about making something from the user's body. They often have noticeable signs or methods of production.

Tier 0:

  • Waterworks: Produce an outrageous flow of tears like garden hoses.
  • Lighter: Produce a flame over your thumb by snapping fingers.
  • Uppercrust: Create and spread a hard, jagged material predominantly made of keratin from your hands.
  • Hay Fever: Produce a pollen-like powder that triggers allergy symptoms in others.
  • Bishie: Produce sparkling lights around your head and body, making you more attractive.
  • Little Pompeii: Produce and control ashes from your fingertips.
  • Static: Charge an internal battery by rubbing body parts, then manipulate electricity internally or externally.
  • Scarface: Have your voice originate from any point within a radius of ten meters.
  • Ice Cube: Produce perfectly chilled ice cubes by shaking a closed fist.
  • Slide & Glide: Produce a force from your body with attractive and repulsive properties, allowing seamless sliding.

Tier 1:

  • Cemedine: Expel a large volume of glue-like, viscous fluid that quickly hardens.
  • Urn Pell: Produce a clay-like substance that quickly sets into ceramic-like objects.
  • Lumber: Generate wood from your body and control it as an extension.
  • Navel Laser: Project extremely powerful lasers from your stomach/belly button.
  • Blackwhip: Generate tendrils of black energy, commanding them through will and physical force.
  • Bad Breath: Project a dense, light-blocking cloud of multicolored fog from your face.
  • Perfume Bubble: Create bubbles filled with any scent you've smelled, with properties varying by mental state.
  • Crystallization: Produce crystals from anywhere on your body for crude armor and weaponry.
  • Man of Iron: Conjure or dismiss a full set of iron armor around your body, with energy blasts from palms.
  • Stockpile: Generate and store power in your Quirk to enhance physical abilities over time.

Tier 2:

  • Acid: Produce corrosive fluids from your skin, adjusting solubility, viscosity, and acidity.
  • Clones: Expel glowing, ectoplasm-like slime that can be transformed into copies of yourself.
  • Explosion: Excrete nitroglycerin from your palms and ignite it to make it explode.
  • Queen Bee: Produce bees with syringes for abdomens, commanding them as an extension for fluid transfer and spying.
  • Somnambulist: Produce a highly potent, sleep-inducing aroma from your body.
  • Easter Eggs: Conjure small, colorful eggs with varying effects and detonation conditions.
  • Horn Cannon: Passively grow curved horns that can be detached and controlled remotely.
  • Glamour: Emit a mist from your mouth that can be manipulated to form complex visual and auditory illusions.
  • Toxic Chainsaw: Generate copious amounts of deadly cytotoxic venom and rotate it at high speeds for slicing.
  • Shield: Generate an array of rapidly multiplying, hexagonal plates over and from your body, forming armor or bladed armaments.

Tier 3:

  • Black Hole: Generate small black holes at your fingertips that suck up and disintegrate matter.
  • Creation: Produce inanimate objects from any exposed part of your body with exacting understanding of atomic structure.
  • Gamma Maximum: Spit glowing, green slime that emits radiation, causing invertebrates to grow hundreds of times their size.
  • Warp Gate: Generate and manipulate a black fog that links two points of space, allowing teleportation.
  • Wave Motion: Convert your vitality into pure energy and project it as spiraling shockwaves.
  • Edge Master: Create and project bladed objects from your body with incredibly high quality iron alloy.
  • Comic: Vocalize onomatopoeia to manifest giant letters with varying effects (e.g., "Blam!" for crashing, "Boing" for elastic).
  • Hatcher: Regurgitate eggs that hatch into obedient 'monsters' with traits reflecting what was consumed.
  • Double: Create copies of yourself or others with almost full capabilities, including Quirks.
  • Toy Soldiers: Eject rapidly expanding clockwork automatons from your chest, inheriting your intelligence and completing orders.

Augmentation Quirks Examples

Augmentation Quirks are all about manipulating, enhancing, and controlling your own body for a variety of effects, either part of it or the whole thing.

Tier 0:

  • Permeation: Become intangible, causing everything to pass through you harmlessly.
  • Blade Arms: Sprout small, short metal blades from your arms.

  • Badger: Lengthen and harden your nails on command for various uses.
  • Gyrate: Rotate your body parts independently at high speeds.
  • Wall Run: Feel heightened gravity in the direction your feet are facing, allowing easier climbing or extending leaps.
  • Suicide Bomb: Explode with incredible force equivalent to your weight in TNT (lethal to user).
  • Switch Up: Flip a mental switch to coordinate perfectly in reverse, increasing flexibility.

Tier 1:

  • Blood Control: Manipulate your own blood (move, shape, harden, liquefy) with less deleterious health effects.
  • Bone Spear: Sprout super hard drills of bone from your body for offense and defense.
  • Hypertrophy: Greatly enlarge one of your body parts at a time, vastly increasing strength and durability.
  • Roughest: Manipulate the texture and properties of your own skin (rougher, smoother, softer, tougher, more elastic).
  • Black: Merge with anything seen as the color black, including shadows, and move through it quickly.
  • Tongue Web: Strengthen, extend, expand, and branch your tongue to an incredible extent.
  • Sugar Rush: Multiply your strength five times over for three minutes for every ten grams of sugar ingested.
  • Air Walk: Stand on and control the air around you, granting levitation and allowing you to run/jump on air.
  • Transform: Transform and take the appearance of another person after consuming their blood.
  • Copy: Copy the Quirks of others by touching them, gaining basic understanding and temporary use.

Tier 2:

  • Blade Tooth: Enlarge, reshape, sharpen, and manipulate your teeth to form dangerous, branching, flexible blades.
  • Foldabody: Thin and stretch your body, taking the form of thread or folding yourself like origami.
  • Harpoon: Project steel spikes on lines resembling rope from your body at bullet-like speeds, with retracting barbs.
  • Tool Arms: Sprout additional arms from your upper body, ending with various tools (saw, screwdriver, chainsaw).
  • Strongarm: Rotate and move shoulders at extreme speed and power, allowing pulverizing punches.
  • The Hand: Summon a massive hand from your body that mimics your dominant hand, floating and crawling at high speeds.
  • Lizard Tail Splitter: Split your body apart into pieces, manipulating them through thought.
  • Muscle Augmentation: Manipulate and amplify your muscle fibers, allowing them to protrude and increase strength, speed, and resilience.
  • Transforming Arms: Radically manipulate the shape of your arms (stretching, bending, expanding, splitting) into wings, whips, etc.
  • Double Time: 'Overclock' two organs or body parts at a time, multiplying their functionality.

Tier 3:

  • Gigantification: Instantly grow to about 68 meters in height, gaining incredible destructive force.
  • Temperature Modulation: Survive extreme temperatures and manipulate your own body's temperature to melt steel or freeze water.
  • Manifest: Manifest body parts and/or characteristics of any animal you eat.
  • Transfer: Transmute your body into any inanimate material by touching it, and merge with it for high-speed travel.
  • Fat Absorption: Manipulate body fat to absorb tangible objects and kinetic forces, discharging accumulated energy in a strike.
  • Ring Fingers: Transform fingers into metal rings, controlling them remotely and enforcing will on victims.
  • Overclock: Hasten perception of time, allowing multiple simulations of potential futures within seconds.
  • Myth: Transform into a larger, more powerful mythological creature, gaining physiological changes and abilities.
  • Stress: Convert anger, frustration, and general stress into raw power, enhancing body parts or projecting outwards.
  • Cow Catcher: Acquire a semi-translucent energy coating that builds momentum, making you an unstoppable force.

Alteration Quirks Examples

Alteration Quirks are normally strange close-range Quirks that carry powerful effects or affect the way things work. They can be physically demanding or hard to control.

Tier 0:

  • Love: Enhance the strength, speed, and resilience of a single individual by declaring your love for them.
  • Coating: Smooth and polish whatever you touch, giving it a slippery, shiny, glossy quality.
  • Graffiti: Alter the color of whatever a short, conical beam touches.
  • Barbs: Sprout small barbs or thorns from inanimate objects you're touching.
  • Sunburn: Grab and peel off a thin, top surface of inanimate objects as a translucent sheet.
  • Soda Fountain: Turn water into another fluid at a whim, based on the last eight liquids ingested.
  • Mochi Press: Crush and bind substances and materials into a cohesive, dough-like mass with your tail.
  • Negative: Alter the way objects or persons you touch interact with the visible light spectrum, rendering them as a photo-negative.
  • Glowstick: Crack your joints to 'paint' surfaces with a glow-in-the-dark quality.
  • Finger Trap: Embed snare traps made of paper by poking an object.

Tier 1:

  • Vertebrae: Project spine-like tentacles from your back that inject a muscle-locking toxic fluid.
  • Magnetism: Magnetize people around you, causing them to behave as if extremely powerful magnets.
  • Weld: Seamlessly 'weld' inanimate objects together at the atomic level.
  • Shake: Vibrate whatever you touch, adjusting strength and speed of vibration.
  • Water Balloon: Alter the surface tension of liquids, making them hard, rubbery, soft, or abrasive.
  • Lock Down: Lock objects in space, immobilizing them by 'turning a key' with your finger.
  • Scissors: Cleanly cut through almost anything with your body by mimicking the shape and motion of scissors.
  • Mimicry: Merge yourself with inanimate objects, then manipulate or shape them.
  • Solid Air: Solidify air, creating static platforms, shields, or other shapes.
  • 3D Printer: Transform existing objects or materials into any object you've previously 'memorized.'

Tier 2:

  • Heal: Rapidly accelerate a target's natural healing by kissing them.
  • Softening: Soften anything you touch until it becomes a fluid, mud-like consistency.
  • Zero Gravity: Completely shut off the influence of gravity upon a target using finger pads.
  • Skateboard: Sprout wheels from whatever you touch and command them to spin at high speeds.
  • Need For Speed: Slow or accelerate any moving object you come in contact with.
  • Elasticity: Bestow elasticity to whatever you touch, including the ground, air, or buildings.
  • Dead Zone: Reduce the electrical conductivity of everything in an area to zero.
  • Twin Impact: Create secondary impacts at the site of any observed impact, many times stronger than the initial.
  • Decay: Destroy virtually anything you lay your hands on, causing it to rapidly crumble to dust.
  • Displacement: Transform arms into huge, webbed hands that displace anything 'swiped' into an orb.

Tier 3:

  • Compress: Shrink anything down inside a spherical prison resembling a marble.
  • Blackjack: Forcibly bend anything you grab up to 90 degrees without compromising structural integrity.
  • Drain: Create a 'drain' on whatever you touch, sucking in moisture and pumping it into a target.
  • Noise: Apply a Disruptive field (scrambles electronic signals) or an Attractive field (charges target with electricity) by pointing.
  • Melting Point: Dramatically alter the boiling point or freezing point of any inanimate object in sight.
